02 · SaaS · Scheduling Platform

Designing a scheduling tool
businesses actually want to use.

Folowr is a B2B scheduling and client management platform built from scratch. Before a single wireframe, I spent time understanding how service businesses actually manage bookings today, where things break, and what they wish existed.

Role
UI/UX Designer (Sole Designer)
Duration
Aug 2025 – Present
Deliverables
30+ screens · 200+ components
Status
Live at folowr.app ↗

The Problem

Most scheduling tools are built for the tool, not the business using it.

I started by researching two competing scheduling platforms before drawing a single screen. What I found was that most tools optimise for feature volume — they pack in everything possible but make the core workflows unnecessarily complex.

Service businesses — coaches, consultants, therapists, trainers — need a tool that gets out of the way and lets them focus on their clients, not their software. That became the design north star for Folowr.

The Core Screens

Hover to pause · Drag to explore

Key Decisions

Clarity over completeness at every decision point.

The calendar view needed to show enough information to be useful at a glance — date, client name, service type, time — without becoming a wall of data. I tested different information hierarchies before settling on a layout that prioritises what matters most: who you're seeing and when.

The bookings table communicates status across four states (confirmed, pending, cancelled, not paid) using both colour and text labels — nothing relies on colour alone. The client management flow was simplified to the essentials: name, contact, notes. Nothing the business doesn't actually use.

The Design System

200+ components built to scale as the product grows.

As the sole designer on this product, I knew the design system would carry the entire visual consistency burden. I built 200+ reusable components in Figma — buttons, form fields, status chips, table rows, modals, navigation states — documented with usage guidelines so engineering could implement without needing to interpret design decisions.

The purple brand colour was chosen to feel distinctive in a market where most scheduling tools default to blue or green — professional but with personality.
