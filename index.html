Case Study · Nexapay

Making a financial decision
feel less like a form.

The account tier upgrade flow was the most complex journey I owned at Nexapay. Users had to complete regulatory requirements to access higher transaction limits — but compliance language and design that feels cold are two different things.

The Problem

Users didn't understand why they needed to upgrade — or what they'd gain.

When I analysed the account upgrade journey, the main friction wasn't the steps themselves — it was that users arrived at the flow without understanding the value exchange. They were asked to submit personal documents (NIN slip, selfie) without a clear sense of what they'd get in return, or why it was necessary. The compliance requirement felt like a wall, not a gate to something better. My job was to reframe it.

Tier Overview

Requirements

NIN Verification

Selfie Capture

What I Did

Lead the user toward the decision, not just through the steps.

I redesigned the entry point to show users exactly where they were (Tier 1), what they were missing (higher limits, more balance), and what it would take to get there — before asking for anything. The tier comparison screen became a clarity tool, not a compliance notice.

Each subsequent step (NIN entry, selfie capture) was framed as progress toward something, with clear language about why each piece of information was needed and how it would be protected. The goal was to make a regulatory requirement feel like a product feature.

Beyond the Upgrade Flow

The Cards screen — balance, transactions, and quick actions in one place.

The Cards screen was designed to give users a complete picture of their financial activity without overwhelming them. Balance prominently displayed, card visual as a trust anchor, and recent transactions immediately visible — all actionable with Top-Up and Withdraw a single tap away.
