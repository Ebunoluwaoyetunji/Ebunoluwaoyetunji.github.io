04 · SaaS · AI Platform

Making AI decisions feel
transparent, not mysterious.

Boulote is an AI-powered talent marketplace connecting professionals and companies. As the designer on the admin platform, my challenge was to make the AI's shortlisting decisions visible, legible, and trustworthy — without oversimplifying what the system was actually doing.

Role
UI/UX Designer
Platform
Admin Dashboard · SaaS
Key Feature
AI shortlisting & job management

The Problem

AI matching is only useful if the person using it trusts it.

The core challenge with Boulote's admin platform wasn't the matching algorithm — it was the interface around it. If admins couldn't understand why the AI had selected a particular professional, or what to do when it hadn't found a match, the feature would be ignored in favour of manual selection.

I needed to design an experience that made the AI feel like a tool with reasoning, not a black box with outputs. That meant surfacing the right information at each stage — match scores, processing status, candidate data — in a way that supported decision-making rather than replacing it.

The Job Management Flow

Key Decisions

Four states, each designed to tell its own story.

The job management flow has four distinct states: Pending Requests (jobs waiting to be processed), AI Review (shortlisting in progress or complete), Ongoing Jobs (matched and active), and Completed. Each state required different information hierarchy and different actions available to the admin.

The "No AI Match" state was particularly important to get right — when the algorithm couldn't find a qualified professional, the admin needed to understand why (missing skills, niche requirements) and have a clear path to manual selection without feeling like the system had failed them. The warm highlight and "Manual select from Database" CTA was designed to make this feel like a feature, not an error.

Two-Sided Design

One platform, two completely different mental models.

Boulote serves both companies posting jobs and professionals applying for them. While my work focused primarily on the admin and company-side experience, every decision had to account for how it would affect the professional's side of the transaction.

The AI match score (displayed as a percentage on each candidate card) was a deliberate transparency choice — giving admins a concrete signal to evaluate rather than just a ranked list. The 94% average match score shown on the AI Review tab was designed to build trust in the system over time, making the AI feel reliable rather than random.
